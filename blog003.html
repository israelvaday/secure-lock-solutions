What to Do When Locked Out

It happens to everyone eventually. You're standing outside your home or car, realizing your keys are on the other side of a locked door. Here's how to handle it calmly and safely.

Step 1: Stay Calm

Panic leads to bad decisions. Take a deep breath. This is an inconvenience, not an emergency (unless someone is trapped inside or there's another urgent situation).

Step 2: Check All Entry Points

Before calling anyone, double-check:

  • All doors (front, back, side, garage)
  • Windows that might be unlocked (ground floor only!)
  • Do you have a spare key somewhere? Neighbor, family member, hidden?
  • Is anyone else home who can let you in?

Step 3: Assess Your Situation

Consider:

  • Are you in a safe location?
  • Is someone (child, pet, elderly person) locked inside?
  • Is the car running with keys inside?
  • What's the weather like?

If there's immediate danger, call 911 first.

Step 4: Call a Licensed Locksmith

A professional locksmith can open most doors without damage. When you call:

  • Describe the type of lock/door
  • Give your exact location
  • Ask for upfront pricing
  • Verify they're licensed

What NOT to Do

Don't Try to Break In

Breaking a window costs more to repair than a locksmith call. Plus, neighbors might call the police on you.

Don't Use YouTube "Lock Picking" Tips

Amateur attempts usually damage the lock, making it more expensive to fix. Leave it to professionals.

Don't Call an Unlicensed "Locksmith"

Scam "locksmiths" are common. They quote low prices on the phone, then charge hundreds more once they arrive. Warning signs:

  • Won't give a business name
  • Arrives in unmarked vehicle
  • Price increases dramatically on arrival
  • Insists on drilling (unnecessary 95% of the time)

If Locked Out of Your Car

  • Check if you have roadside assistance (AAA, insurance, car manufacturer)
  • If the car is running, this is urgent—call a locksmith immediately
  • Modern cars are harder to open—don't damage your vehicle trying

Prevent Future Lockouts

  • Keep a spare key with a trusted neighbor or family member
  • Consider a smart lock with keypad backup
  • Don't hide keys under doormats (burglars check there first)
  • Save a locksmith's number in your phone before you need it
